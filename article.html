AI in Development Work

A Critical Reflection on Tools, Skills, and Professional Integrity

The Wedding Documentation Scenario

The Situation:

You are organizing your wedding. A documentation team approaches you with the following offer:

  • Price: ₱40,000 for the complete wedding package
  • Equipment: iPhone 16 Pro Max for video and photo coverage
  • Editing Software: CapCut for same-day edit video
  • Experience: 2 years in the wedding documentation business
  • Portfolio: Above average quality work samples
The Question:

Would you agree to this deal? Explain your answer and justify the integrity of their work.

Critical Analysis

At first glance, many people might hesitate. The thought process often goes:

"They're using an iPhone and CapCut? Those are consumer-grade tools! For ₱40,000, shouldn't they be using professional cinema cameras and Adobe Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve?"

This reaction reveals a common misconception: equating professional quality with expensive tools. But is this fair? Is this the right way to evaluate their service?

The iPhone 16 Pro Max has a camera system that rivals professional equipment in many scenarios, and CapCut, while accessible, offers powerful editing capabilities. The tools themselves aren't the issue; it's how they're used.

When you hire a wedding documentation team, you're not just paying for equipment. You're paying for:

Artistic Vision

The ability to see and capture meaningful moments, compose beautiful shots, and tell your story visually.

Technical Expertise

Understanding lighting, angles, timing, and how to maximize their equipment's capabilities.

Experience & Reliability

2 years of experience means they've handled various wedding scenarios, challenges, and client expectations.

Post-Production Skills

The ability to edit footage into a compelling narrative, with proper pacing, music, and emotional impact.

Professionalism

Punctuality, communication, backup plans, and the ability to work under pressure on your special day.

Emotional Intelligence

Reading the room, anticipating moments, and capturing genuine emotions without being intrusive.

Key Insight: Their portfolio is "above average," which means they've demonstrated they can deliver quality results with their chosen tools. The proof is in their work, not their equipment list.

Answer: Yes, I would agree to this deal.

1. Tools Don't Define Quality, Skills Do

The iPhone 16 Pro Max is a remarkably capable device with professional-grade features including ProRes video, advanced computational photography, and excellent low-light performance. CapCut, while accessible, offers professional editing features. What matters is how effectively they use these tools.

2. Portfolio Speaks Louder Than Equipment

An "above average" portfolio means they've consistently delivered quality work. This is tangible evidence of their capability. A photographer with a $10,000 camera but poor skills will produce worse results than a skilled photographer with a smartphone.

3. Experience Matters

Two years of wedding documentation experience means they've navigated various challenges: difficult lighting, unpredictable weather, demanding clients, technical issues, and the pressure of capturing once-in-a-lifetime moments. This experience is invaluable.

4. Modern Tools Are Democratizing Quality

Technology has advanced to the point where consumer-grade tools can produce professional results in skilled hands. This democratization means quality is more accessible, and clients can get excellent service at better prices.

5. Value for Money

₱40,000 for comprehensive wedding documentation with proven quality (based on their portfolio) represents good value. You're paying for their expertise, not equipment depreciation.

The Bottom Line

Judge service providers by their demonstrated results and professional competence, not by the brand names of their tools. The integrity of their work is validated by their portfolio and client satisfaction, not by their equipment budget.

The Parallel: "Vibe Coders" and AI-Assisted Development

The Scenario Mirrors Modern Development
Wedding Photographers
  • Use accessible tools (iPhone, CapCut)
  • Leverage technology to enhance their work
  • Focus on vision and execution
  • Judged by portfolio results
  • Experience guides tool usage
AI-Assisted Developers
  • Use AI tools (Copilot, ChatGPT)
  • Leverage AI to enhance productivity
  • Focus on architecture and problem-solving
  • Judged by code quality and outcomes
  • Experience guides AI prompting
Reflecting on "Vibe Coders"
What is a "Vibe Coder"?

The term "vibe coder" often refers (sometimes pejoratively) to developers who rely heavily on AI assistance, with critics suggesting they lack deep technical understanding. But is this criticism fair?

The Critical Perspective

Concerns about AI-heavy development:

  • Shallow Understanding: Developers might not understand the code they're using
  • Dependency: Over-reliance on AI could atrophy problem-solving skills
  • Quality Issues: AI-generated code might have security vulnerabilities or inefficiencies
  • Debugging Challenges: Difficulty troubleshooting code you didn't fully write
  • Professional Integrity: Questions about what constitutes "real" programming skill
The Balanced Perspective

Valid points about AI-assisted development:

  • Productivity Multiplier: AI helps developers work faster and tackle more complex projects
  • Learning Tool: AI can teach best practices and expose developers to new patterns
  • Focus on Higher-Level Thinking: Developers can focus on architecture and problem-solving rather than syntax
  • Democratization: More people can participate in software development
  • Industry Evolution: Just as IDEs and Stack Overflow changed development, AI is the next evolution
Integrity, Creativity, and Skill Level

Integrity: A developer's integrity isn't compromised by using AI tools, just as a photographer's isn't compromised by using computational photography. Integrity is about:

  • Delivering what you promise
  • Being honest about your capabilities
  • Taking responsibility for your code
  • Continuing to learn and improve
  • Using tools ethically and appropriately

Creativity: AI doesn't eliminate creativity; it can enhance it. Creative developers use AI to:

  • Explore more solution approaches quickly
  • Prototype ideas faster
  • Focus mental energy on novel problems
  • Learn new techniques and patterns

Skill Level: The skill isn't in typing every character; it's in:

  • Knowing what to build and why
  • Designing robust architectures
  • Making informed technology choices
  • Understanding trade-offs and implications
  • Reviewing and improving AI-generated code
  • Solving problems AI can't handle

The Conclusion

Just as you would hire the wedding photographers based on their portfolio and proven ability to deliver quality work regardless of whether they use an iPhone or a cinema camera, you should evaluate developers based on their ability to deliver working, maintainable, and effective solutions.

Final Reflection

"The wedding photographers with an iPhone and CapCut can deliver beautiful, memorable documentation of your special day. Similarly, 'vibe coders' using AI tools can deliver effective software solutions. In both cases, what matters isn't the sophistication of the tools, but the skill, experience, creativity, and integrity of the professional wielding them. Don't confuse the tool with the craftsperson—and don't let tool snobbery blind you to genuine talent and capability."
